Stowable Shopping Cart Project

    As an introduction to the Senior Design Project, students were tasked with designing a stowable shopping cart which utilizes a folding mechanism comparable to that used on ambulance stretchers which would allow the shopping cart to be easily loaded into the rear of an SUV or minivan, eliminating the need to transfer the contents of the cart. The design parameters for this project were:
  • The cart shall have a cargo container (“basket”) with a base of 16 x 24 inches and 12 inches deep for holding groceries or other goods up to 30 lb.
  • The cart shall easily slide into the back of the vehicle with a deck height of 30 inches without requiring the user to do any lifting.
  • The stowed volume of the cart, including the cargo container, shall not exceed 30 x 30 x 18 inches (18 inches in height).
  • The unloaded cart shall weigh less than 30 lb.
  • The time required to load or unload the loaded cart from the vehicle shall not exceed 45 seconds.
  • The portion of the cart that contacts the vehicle shall be designed not to damage the vehicle.
  • The cart shall be simple, safe, reliable and aesthetically pleasing.
The design I created in Solidworks for this project was heavily influenced by an ambulance gurney, focusing on the folding aspect of the cart. This project was a great learning exercise and played a large role in teaching me the Solidworks skills required to obtain my Solidworks Associate Certification.

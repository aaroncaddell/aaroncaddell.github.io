Automobile Modeling Project

    The Automobile Modeling project was a linear systems and controls project in which students derived an equation of motion for an automobile then linearized the equation and studied the differences between the linearized and non-linearized models, utilizing Matlab. This project also involved studying the cruise control of an automobile with both linear and nonlinear equations of motion which allowed students to observe that when a 10 mph increase is applied as a step input to a cruise control currently at a constant speed (65 mph) the nonlinear model sees a faster response. This project also had a section in which students derived differential equations for the suspension of an automobile. Students then find transfer functions relating the road surface to the sprung mass, unsprung mass, and tire deflection and determine step, frequency, and time response for each of the transfer functions. While this was one of the most challenging undergraduate projects it was an excellent application of linear systems.
